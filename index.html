Work in progress

Dani Arribas Bel, Alba Miñano Mañero, Souneil Park, Diego Puga
November 2022

City buzz: Face-to-Face interactions and the urban environment

We measure face-to-face interactions as the coincidence in space and time of two cellphone users who have previously called each other or who start doing so after the event. We characterise the quantity (interactions per unit of time), quality (network centrality of contacts met), and variety (probability that two interactions are with different contacts) of face-to-face interactions for every individual-location pair. We also examine how different features of the urban environment affect interactions and consider assortative matching and complementarities of individuals and locations.
Alba Miñano Mañero
November 2022

The park movement and the urban mortality transition

This project studies how the improvements in the urban physical space (the park movement) implemented between 1850 and 1950 contributed to the urban mortality transition in the City of New York. Although these landscapes modifications were motivated by incorrect science (such as reducing miasmas), they may have had positive long-term effects.
